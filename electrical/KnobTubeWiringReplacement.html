Knob and Tube Wiring Replacement Services

What is Knob and Tube Wiring?

Knob and tube wiring is an older electrical system once common in homes built before modern electrical standards. It can be unsafe today because of aging insulation and outdated capacity.

How do I know if my home has it?

We inspect attics, basements, and accessible wall areas to confirm whether knob and tube wiring is present. If you are not sure, schedule an inspection for a clear assessment.

Problems With Knob and Tube Wiring

1. Fire Hazard

Aging insulation and heat buildup can create a serious fire risk in older electrical systems.

2. Unsafe Modifications

Over time, add-ons and repairs can expose wiring and increase the chance of electrical shocks or failure.

3. No Grounding

Knob and tube wiring typically lacks grounding, which is required for modern safety standards and appliance protection.

Modern homes rely on high-demand appliances, EV chargers, and smart systems. Upgrading knob and tube wiring ensures your home stays safe and reliable.

Why Upgrading Knob and Tube Wiring Matters

Safer Electrical System

New wiring improves safety, reduces fire risk, and meets code requirements.

Improved Capacity

Updated wiring supports modern appliances and higher electrical loads without strain.

Peace of Mind

Know your home is protected with reliable, professionally installed wiring.

What Should I Do If My Home Has Knob and Tube Wiring?

If your house has knob and tube wiring, you do not always have to replace it. But if you want or need to make an electrical upgrade, you should hire a licensed and experienced electrician.

Even though knob and tube wiring does not pose a serious danger, it can be a fire hazard. We can help you evaluate the safest next steps.
